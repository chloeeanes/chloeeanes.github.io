My College Education...So Far

Throughout my time in college, I have learned a wide variety of topics. However, my main focus has been working toward my English degree and completing my minors in dynamic publishing and digital media and web design.

So far, I have obtained an associate of arts degree in liberal arts (with a concentration in English), graduating (as stated on my home page) summa cum laude. Many believe the "detour" to community college is a "waste of time," but throughout my time at NVCC and after transferring to George Mason University, I received a diverse education. Therefore, from teaching styles to material covered, my experiences have covered a wider range of possibilities for what one could experience during their college life.


Some example of courses I completed at both schools are listed below:


Northern Virginia Community College Courses:

These courses covered most of my core requirements, giving me a chance to learn a wide range of subjects.

  • Conversational Chinese II
    • Teaches listening, speaking, writing, and reading in the Chinese language at an intermediate level. Cultural awareness is also touched on constantly.
  • Intro to Human Communication
    • Develops one's ability to carry out stellar communication skills at multiple levels (individual, small group, and public).
  • Physical and Historical Geology
    • These courses covered the building blocks of the Earth along with the evolution of it and its life-forms.
  • Intro to Digital Literature and Computer Applications
    • Builds one's skills in the digital sphere along with explaining the differences among software, hardware, storage systems, databases, and operating systems. Includes some coding in HTML5 and ethical/legal concerns.
  • Cultural Anthropology
    • Covers the origins and development of human cultures over time. Western and non-Western cultures are discussed throughout the course.

George Mason University Courses:

Here, I have taken more courses focused on my major and minors, giving me the opportunity to hone my skills in English research, literary analysis, and composition. This focus also includes my interest in web coding and the arts.

  • New Media in the Creative Arts
    • Builds an understanding of newer artistic technologies while creating multiple projects using said technologies.
  • Advanced Composition
    • Continues to develop writing and rhetorical skills as well as how to best conduct research, concerning analyzing and picking credible sources. Includes learning how to write for various audiences.
  • Digital Design Studio
    • Develops proficiency in Adobe software such as Photoshop, InDesign, and Illustrator. Includes teaching how to build a basic webpage using HTML5.
  • American Women Writers at the Movies
    • Exploring and analyzing screenplays/novels written by Amercian woman. Includes projects analyzing textual elements of screenplays both part of the curriculum and outside of it.
  • Intro to Shakespeare
    • Explores a broad range of Shakespeare's plays across multiple genres such as history, comedy, and drama.

These courses, and many more, have assisted greatly in developing my wide knowledge base as well as my adaptability skills, through diverse content and projects.
